### Age-Related Sleep Changes

Sleep naturally change with age. After 55, it's common to experience:

- Increasingly Earlier bedtimes and wake times
- More frequent nighttime awakenings
- Perceiving sleep as lighter and more disrupted

These changes are normal and you will receive personalized guidance in the sleep report to improve your sleep health and quality of life.

Prior to the age of 25 there is biological tendency for a delayed sleep phase, a preference to stay up late and wake up late. This may conflict with school or work schedules. If you're struggling with early morning obligations, you can shift your schedule. In your sleep report you will receive guidance on next steps to maintain a healthy schedules that does not interfere with you daily activities and improves your sleep health and quality of life.

**For Women >45 years of age:** Sleep disturbance in women in your age group is often related pre-and perimenopausal home related changes. We wll provide you with specific information and guidance regarding these changes.
